 I served four years as a Security Forces Airman in the United States Air Force. To the left are some of the soft skills I developed during my time in uniform, which include "Integrity", "Service", and "Excellence", derived from the three Air Force core values of "Integrity First, Service Before Self, and Excellence in All We Do."
My initial duty assignment was as an Entry Controller. Primary duties in this position include Access Control, Identification Checks, Surveillance, Threat Detection, Emergency Response, and Incident Reporting. These physical helped develop a security mindset, and many of these skill translate well into those used in Cybersecurity, such as User Authentication and Authorization, Monitoring, Anomaly Detection, Network Perimeter Defense, and Collaboration in Security Operations.
After initial training, I was assigned as a Patrolman. This position included responsibilities such as conducting Routine Patrols, Surveillance, Law Enforcement, Traffic Control, Report Writing, and Incident Response. The skills I gained in this role translate to the digital realm well in the areas of Network Monitoring, Threat Detection, Access Control, Policy Enforcement, Cyber Incident Response and Mitigation.
In this position I was selected to attend the USAF Resource Protection and Crime Prevention Theory, Practice, and Management Course, Eastern KentuckyUniversity. This course at EKU was a specialized training program designed for members of the Air Force that focused on equipping personnel with knowledge and skills related to resource protection, crime prevention, and security management, including bypassing physical and technology-based security tools, vulnerability and risk analysis, and leveraging Linux tools to bypass computer systems security in order to collect evidence and intelligence, as well as increased awareness to harden systems against these tools and techniques.
My final roll as a Security Forces member was as a BDOC Controller, or Dispatcher. Skills fine tuned there included Incident Coordination, Communication, Decision Making, Task Prioritization, Logistics, Resrouce Management, Risk Assessment, and Threat Intelligence, which translate to digital counterparts of Cross-Functional Collaboration, Incident Triage, Documentation, Evidence Collection, Incident Response Planning.
