Experience as a Security Forces Airman: I proudly served four years as a Security Forces Airman in the United States Air Force. Below, you'll find a summary of the core soft skills I developed during my service, chiefly: Integrity, Service, and Excellence. These qualities are rooted in the Air Force's core values:

"Integrity First, Service Before Self, and Excellence in All We Do."

Roles and Acquired Competencies

Entry Controller Assignment: My initial role in the Air Force was as an Entry Controller, where I was responsible for access control, identification checks, surveillance, threat detection, and incident reporting. These duties fostered a strong security mindset and directly align with key principles in cybersecurity, such as user authentication and authorization, monitoring, anomaly detection, network perimeter defense, and collaborative security operations.

Deployment Experiences: I had the opportunity to serve on two deployments to Iraq. My first deployment was to Camp Bucca, where I performed law and order duties, ensuring the safety and security of personnel and facilities in a high-pressure environment. My second deployment was an assignment to Camp Stryker in Baghdad, where I served as part of a Police Transition Team. In this role, I worked closely with Iraqi Police forces, training them in essential areas such as community policing, investigative techniques, sensitive site exploitation, and conducting high-risk warrant service. This experience required strong cross-cultural communication skills, adaptability, and the ability to mentor and build operational capabilities within a partner nation’s security force.

Patrolman Responsibilities: Following initial training, I transitioned to the role of Patrolman. This position required me to perform routine patrols, surveillance, law enforcement, traffic control, report writing, and incident response. These experiences equipped me with skills that translate seamlessly to cybersecurity, including network monitoring, threat detection, policy enforcement, and cyber incident response and mitigation.

Specialized Training at Eastern Kentucky University: During my service, I was selected to attend the USAF Resource Protection and Crime Prevention Theory, Practice, and Management Course at Eastern Kentucky University. This specialized program focused on resource protection, crime prevention, and security management. Training covered advanced topics such as bypassing physical and technological security measures, vulnerability and risk analysis, and using Linux tools to bypass system security for evidence collection and intelligence gathering. Additionally, the course emphasized strengthening systems against these techniques, enhancing my awareness of modern security challenges.

Base Defense Operations Center: In my final position as a Security Forces member, I served as a BDOC Controller, or Dispatcher. This role required incident coordination, effective communication, decision-making, task prioritization, logistics, resource management, risk assessment, and threat intelligence. These skills readily translate to digital environments, such as cross-functional collaboration, incident triage, documentation, evidence collection, incident response planning, and alarm monitoring.
